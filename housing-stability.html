Housing Stability, Grip, and Desk Use

Workflow fit. A self-inking office stamp should match the document routine before it matches the supply catalog. Purchase orders, received marks, paid marks, approval steps, address blocks, return labels, and date tracking all need different imprint sizes and wording. For housing stability, grip, and desk use, think through the actual paper path: who picks up the stamp, which documents get marked, how often the wording changes, where the stamp is stored, and how the mark must look when the file is reviewed later. Strong buying notes are specific: measure the form space, choose the exact phrase, confirm ink color, check whether date bands are needed, and decide who replaces pads before the imprint fades. Also consider the busiest day in the office, because a stamp that feels fine for one envelope may be irritating when fifty forms need the same clean mark.
